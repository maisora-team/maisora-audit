The Problem

You don't have a marketing problem. You have a diagnosis problem.

When bookings drop, most hosts do the same three things: lower the price, take new photos, or buy another tool. Sometimes one of them works. Usually none of them do — because none of them were built on a diagnosis. They were built on a guess.

The truth is that Airbnb's ranking is driven by a small set of factors that compound. Response time, review velocity, instant book, photo quality, completeness, pricing position against your local set, and a handful of others. If one of them is broken, the others can't compensate. If three of them are broken, you're invisible — and no price drop will fix it.

The fix is to look at your listing the way the algorithm does: factor by factor, with weights, and with a clear order of operations. That's what this audit does. Nothing more.

Free audits are marketing material — they exist to put you in an email funnel. This is a working tool with a weighted scoring model behind it, prioritized fix recommendations, and a revenue-at-stake estimate. There is no upsell, no email sequence, no course.

Do I need to connect my Airbnb account?

No. You answer 17 questions about your listing in plain English. Nothing is connected to Airbnb. Nothing is scraped. Nothing leaves your browser.

How long does the audit take?

About 5 minutes if you know your listing well. Maybe 10 if you have to check a couple of stats in your dashboard.

Does it work outside the U.S.?

Yes. The scoring model is based on Airbnb's global ranking signals — response time, reviews, completeness, instant book, photo quality, pricing position — not on a specific market. It works in any country Airbnb operates in.

Can I run it on more than one listing?

Yes. Your license is per-buyer, not per-listing. Run it on every property you operate, run it again after you fix things, run it on a future property.

Is this a course or a PDF?

Neither. It's an interactive web tool. You answer questions, you get a live scorecard with a gauge, breakdown bars, fix cards, and a projected score. You don't read a 40-page document and try to remember what to do — you fix in order.

What if my score doesn't change?
